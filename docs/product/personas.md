# Personas

## 1. Cardiology Fellow

**Role:** 2nd–3rd year cardiology fellowship trainee  
**Context:** Rotating through echocardiography, needs TEE credentialing. Limited supervised procedure time. Studies in evenings and on weekends.  
**Primary need:** Practice identifying and obtaining standard ASE views before supervised cases. Build muscle memory for probe manipulation without patient risk.  
**Key behaviors:** Uses simulator in 15–30 minute sessions. Wants immediate feedback on whether the current view matches the target. Uses mobile or laptop.

## 2. Cardiac Anesthesiology Resident

**Role:** Anesthesiology resident on cardiac rotation  
**Context:** First exposure to TEE. Needs to pass a basic perioperative TEE certification. May have no prior echo background.  
**Primary need:** Structured introduction to TEE anatomy — understanding what the probe sees and why. Probe manipulation is secondary to anatomical understanding.  
**Key behaviors:** Prefers guided walkthrough mode. Reads anatomical labels. Pauses and rotates the 3D view to understand spatial relationships.

## 3. Attending Echocardiographer / Instructor

**Role:** Senior cardiologist or echo lab director who teaches fellows  
**Context:** Runs weekly echo didactics. Wants to show trainees the relationship between probe position and image plane in 3D.  
**Primary need:** A demonstration tool that shows the probe, thoracic anatomy, and resulting image plane simultaneously. Can annotate or highlight structures.  
**Key behaviors:** Uses on a large screen or projector. Wants to quickly jump to any of the 28 standard views. Expects anatomical accuracy.

## 4. Medical Educator / Curriculum Developer

**Role:** Builds TEE curriculum for a training program or medical school  
**Context:** Needs scalable, cost-free tools that trainees can use independently. May have limited technical background.  
**Primary need:** Ability to create and assign structured exercises (e.g., "find ME 4C and label the mitral valve"). Track trainee completion and accuracy.  
**Key behaviors:** Uses web-based admin interface. Wants to customize which views or anatomy are shown per exercise. Expects reliable uptime and no installation barrier.
